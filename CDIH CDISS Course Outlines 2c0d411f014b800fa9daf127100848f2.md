## CDIH Course Outline

## Module 1: Foundational Context and Organizational Structure

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 1.1: Define the mission of key internal and external organizations, including USSTRATCOM, NSA/CSS, DISA, and USCYBERCOM, and describe their coordination with NCDOC
- ELO 1.2: Define the locations and responsibilities for each Combatant Commander (COCOM)
- ELO 1.3: Explain the distinctions between the Navy Area of Responsibility (AOR) and Joint COCOM, as well as the administrative versus tactical Chain of Command
- ELO 1.4: Define the purpose and intended audience for the CJCSM 6510.01 series and SECNAVINST 5239.19A
- ELO 1.5: Define the purpose of the Cyber Security Service Provider (CSSP) Certification and Accreditation process and state the timeframe for recertification

### LES 1.1: Organizations and Relationships

**Key Concepts:**

- Define the mission of key internal and external organizations (e.g., USSTRATCOM, NSA/CSS, DISA, USCYBERCOM, NIOCs, CERT/CC) and their relation/coordination with NCDOC
- Define Combatant Commander (COCOM) locations and responsibilities
- Explain the difference between Navy AOR and Joint COCOM, and the administrative versus tactical Chain of Command
- State the relationship between Incident Handlers and Mission Managers

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 1.1.1: Define the mission of key internal and external organizations (e.g., USSTRATCOM, NSA/CSS, DISA, USCYBERCOM) and describe their coordination with NCDOC
- ELO 1.1.2: Define the locations and responsibilities for each Combatant Commander (COCOM)
- ELO 1.1.3: Explain the distinctions between the Navy Area of Responsibility (AOR) and Joint COCOM
- ELO 1.1.4: State the relationship between Incident Handlers and Mission Managers
- ELO 1.1.5: Explain the difference between the administrative and tactical Chain of Command

### LES 1.2: Governing Documents and Compliance

**Key Concepts:**

- Define the purpose and audience for critical documents (e.g., CJCSM 6510.01 series, SECNAVINST 5239.19A)
- Define the purpose and process of the Cyber Security Service Provider (CSSP) Certification and Accreditation
- State NCDOC's network responsibilities and define the NCDOC Cyber Policy Matrix

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 1.2.1: Define the purpose and intended target audience for the CJCSM 6510.01 series
- ELO 1.2.2: Define the purpose and intended target audience for SECNAVINST 5239.19A
- ELO 1.2.3: Define the purpose of the Cyber Security Service Provider (CSSP) Certification and Accreditation process
- ELO 1.2.4: State NCDOC's network responsibilities and define the NCDOC Cyber Policy Matrix

## Module 2: Technical Review

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 2.1: List and describe the seven layers of the OSI model and the four layers of the TCP/IP model
- ELO 2.2: Identify IP address classes, reserved ranges, Network Address Translation (NAT), and the purpose of Classless Inter-Domain Routing (CIDR)
- ELO 2.3: Define reconnaissance techniques, including fingerprinting, footprinting, port scanning, and banner grabbing
- ELO 2.4: Describe types of spoofing, including IP address, ARP cache, and Web spoofing
- ELO 2.5: List and describe at least four indicators of a computer virus infection and explain how email can be malicious

### LES 2.1: Networking Review

**Key Concepts:**

- List and describe the 7 layers of the OSI model and the 4 layers of the TCP/IP model
- Identify IP address classes, reserved ranges, NAT, subnet masks, and CIDR
- Demonstrate high-level understanding of network security architecture concepts

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 2.1.1: List and describe the seven layers of the OSI model and the four layers of the TCP/IP model
- ELO 2.1.2: Identify IP address classes, reserved ranges, and Network Address Translation (NAT)
- ELO 2.1.3: Define the purpose of a subnet mask and Classless Inter-Domain Routing (CIDR)

### LES 2.2: Vulnerabilities, Attacks, and Exploits Review

**Key Concepts:**

- Define reconnaissance techniques (e.g., Fingerprinting, Footprinting, Active/Passive reconnaissance, Port scanning, Banner grabbing)
- Describe various types of spoofing (IP address, ARP cache, Web)
- Explain how email can be malicious and list indicators of computer virus infection

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 2.2.1: Define reconnaissance techniques, including fingerprinting, footprinting, port scanning, and banner grabbing
- ELO 2.2.2: Describe types of spoofing, including IP address, ARP cache, and Web spoofing
- ELO 2.2.3: Explain how email can be malicious and list indicators of a computer virus infection

## Module 3: Core Incident Handling Process

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 3.1: Define Computer Network Defense (CND) standard terms, including Attack Vector, Exploit, Incident, Vulnerability, and Malicious Logic
- ELO 3.2: List and define CND Categories and explain the difference between CND Categories and the Order of Precedence
- ELO 3.3: Identify and explain the six primary phases of the Incident Handling Methodology and Life Cycle

- ELO 3.4: Explain the flow of an incident from notification to ticket closure
- ELO 3.5: Define positive contact and state the required timeframes for contact with a command based on CND Category
- ELO 3.6: Define Chain of Custody and describe the required actions when notified that a hard drive has been shipped or arrived

### LES 3.1: IH Fundamentals and Methodology

**Key Concepts:**

- Define CND standard terms (e.g., Attack Vector, Exploit, Incident, Vulnerability, Malicious Logic)
- List and define CND Categories and explain the difference between CND Categories and Order of Precedence
- Define the Incident Handling Methodology and Life Cycle and identify its six primary phases
- Define IATO, ATO, and C&A, including who manages them and their approval timeframes

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 3.1.1: Define CND standard terms, including Attack Vector, Exploit, Incident, Vulnerability, and Malicious Logic
- ELO 3.1.2: List and define CND Categories and explain the difference between CND Categories and Order of Precedence
- ELO 3.1.3: Identify and explain the six primary phases of the Incident Handling Methodology and Life Cycle
- ELO 3.1.4: Define IATO, ATO, and C&A, including who manages them and their approval timeframes

### LES 3.2: Incident Management Flow

**Key Concepts:**

- Explain the flow for an incident from notification to ticket closure
- Define positive contact and state the required timeframes for contact with a command based on CND Category
- Detail the minimum required information for creating a ticket and what information is contained in the Work Details
- Explain escalation procedures for non-responsive commands and the difference between acceptable and unacceptable 24-hour updates

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 3.2.1: Explain the flow of an incident from notification to ticket closure
- ELO 3.2.2: Define positive contact and state the required timeframes for contact with a command based on CND Category
- ELO 3.2.3: Detail the minimum required information for creating a ticket and what information is contained in the Work Details
- ELO 3.2.4: Explain escalation procedures for non-responsive commands and the difference between acceptable and unacceptable 24-hour updates

### LES 3.3: Chain of Custody

**Key Concepts:**

- Define Chain of Custody and explain the importance of maintaining a proper log
- Describe the required actions when IH is notified that a hard drive has been shipped or has arrived

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 3.3.1: Define Chain of Custody and explain the importance of maintaining a proper log
- ELO 3.3.2: Describe the required actions when IH is notified that a hard drive has been shipped or has arrived

### LES 3.4: Internal Coordination

**Key Concepts:**

- Define how key personnel will interact and communicate with Incident Handling

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 3.4.1: Define how key watchfloor cells (AS&W, Intel, DCO DIRSUPP, HBSS, DCOWO, BWC) integrate with Incident Handling

### LAB 3.5: Incident Management Flow

**Key Concepts:**

- Given an inciting event, generate Investigations, and assign tasks
- Coordinate investigative tasking amongst key personnel
- Ensure SOP compliance during investigative process

## Module 4: Communications and Supporting Systems

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 4.1: Identify the five steps for successful technical writing and explain the importance of accuracy and conciseness
- ELO 4.2: List the minimum information required when notifying a command about an incident and describe methods to locate a command's Plain Language Address (PLA)
- ELO 4.3: Discuss the function, mandatory fields, and status options for a Problem Investigation (PBI) and Problem Known Error (PKE) in ITSM
- ELO 4.4: State the correlation between ITSM and the Joint Incident Management System (JIMS) and identify which ITSM fields replicate to JIMS
- ELO 4.5: Define Cyber Core and SMART Reporting, and demonstrate the ability to call up a dashboard or report

### LES 4.1: Technical Communication

**Key Concepts:**

- Identify the five steps for successful technical writing and explain the importance of accuracy, conciseness, audience, and scope
- List methods for command notification and the minimum information to provide upon contact
- List ways to obtain a Point of Contact (POC) and locate or verify a command's Plain Language Address (PLA)

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 4.1.1: Identify the five steps for successful technical writing and explain the importance of accuracy and conciseness
- ELO 4.1.2: List the minimum information required when notifying a command about an incident
- ELO 4.1.3: Describe methods to obtain a Point of Contact (POC) and locate or verify a command's Plain Language Address (PLA)

### LES 4.2: Reporting and Messaging

**Key Concepts:**

- Explain how various reports are handled (e.g., CER, IIR, Tippers, TASKORDs/FRAGOs)
- Define NCDOC Alerts, Forensics Summary Reports (FSR), and OPREP-3s, including who has release authority for outgoing messages

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 4.2.1: Explain how reports such as CER, IIR, and Tippers are handled
- ELO 4.2.2: Define NCDOC Alerts, Forensics Summary Reports (FSR), and OPREP-3s, and identify release authority

### LES 4.3: ITSM

**Key Concepts:**

- Discuss the function, components, and mandatory fields of a Problem Investigation (PBI) and Problem Known Error (PKE)
- Understand the ITSM tabs, status/action options, and Work Info Type options

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 4.3.1: Discuss the function, mandatory fields, and components of a Problem Investigation (PBI) and Problem Known Error (PKE)
- ELO 4.3.2: Identify ITSM tabs, status/action options, and Work Info Type options

### LES 4.4: Cyber Core / JIMS

**Key Concepts:**

- Define Cyber Core/SMART Reporting, including dashboards, reports, and customization
- Understand the correlation between ITSM and the Joint Incident Management System (JIMS)
- Identify who manages JIMS, what other agencies replicate data to JIMS, and which ITSM fields replicate

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 4.4.1: Define Cyber Core and SMART Reporting
- ELO 4.4.2: State the correlation between ITSM and the Joint Incident Management System (JIMS) and identify which ITSM fields replicate to JIMS

### LAB 4.5: Triage Handling

**Key Concepts:**

- Demonstrate knowledge of the information contained in key reports and documents (CER/CEL, CTR, FSR, Cyber Alert, etc.)
- Create a PBI in ITSM
- Oversee Triage of an incident

## Module 5: Leadership and Administration

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 5.1: Explain the purpose of the Incident Handling Pass Down Log and the Read and Heed
- ELO 5.2: Define the required steps for all PBIs prior to submission to Final QC and the time threshold for PBIs set to "IH Lead Review"
- ELO 5.3: Describe the process for notifying NCIS of a PBI and identify what incidents warrant notification
- ELO 5.4: Discuss the importance of an open PBI review and identify who conducts it
- ELO 5.5: Describe the roles within the Mission Manager support staff and define positional authority
- ELO 5.6: Discuss the steps for creating, updating, or cancelling a Temporary Standing Order (TSO)
- ELO 5.7: State the necessary actions for adding a new exploit or a new PLA to ITSM
- ELO 5.8: State what is checked during Initial Quality Control (IQC) and Final Quality Control (FQC)
- ELO 5.9: Define the fields within ITSM that result in automatic reopens if found to be incorrect and explain why

### LES 5.1: Incident Handling Leadership

**Key Concepts:**

- Define required steps for all PBIs prior to submission to Final QC
- Define the time threshold for PBIs set to "IH Lead Review"
- Describe Initial Quality Control (IQC) procedures, Actions Required Work Detail entries/timelines
- Describe the process for notifying NCIS of a PBI and what incidents warrant notification

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 5.1.1: Explain the purpose and maintenance of the Incident Handling Pass Down Log and the Read and Heed
- ELO 5.1.2: Define required steps for all PBIs prior to submission to Final QC and the time threshold for "IH Lead Review"
- ELO 5.1.3: Describe Initial Quality Control (IQC) procedures and "Actions Required" Work Detail entries
- ELO 5.1.4: Describe the process for notifying NCIS of a PBI

### LES 5.2: Incident Analysis

**Key Concepts:**

- Discuss the importance of, and who conducts, an open PBI review
- Define positional authority and describe each role within the Mission Manager support staff
- Describe necessary actions for adding a new exploit or a new PLA to ITSM

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 5.2.1: Discuss the importance of an open PBI review and who conducts it
- ELO 5.2.2: Describe the roles within the Mission Manager support staff and define positional authority
- ELO 5.2.3: Discuss the steps for creating, updating, or cancelling a Temporary Standing Order (TSO)
- ELO 5.2.4: State the necessary actions for adding a new exploit or a new PLA to ITSM

### LES 5.3: Quality Assurance

**Key Concepts:**

- State what is checked during Initial Quality Control (IQC) and Final Quality Control (FQC)
- Define ITSM fields that result in automatic reopens if incorrect and state why
- Describe the information contained in the Weekly Quality Control Report

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 5.3.1: State what is checked during Initial Quality Control (IQC) and Final Quality Control (FQC)
- ELO 5.3.2: Define ITSM fields that result in automatic reopens if incorrect and explain why
- ELO 5.3.3: Describe the information contained in the Weekly Quality Control Report and steps to take if there is a disagreement with QC determinations

### LAB 5.4: Incident Management

**Key Concepts:**

- Coordinate IR/IH functions
- Track and Document IR/IH functions
- Oversee incident triage
- Report on incident triage, to include vulnerability analysis and AAR

## Capstone Module 6: CDIH Capstone

### Module Learning Objectives

- ELO 6.1: Demonstrate proficiency with IH Management and Oversight Tasks
- ELO 6.2: Demonstrate proficiency in IH Quality Control
- ELO 6.3: Demonstrate proficiency with IH Reporting
- ELO 6.4: Demonstrate proficiency with IH Briefings
- ELO 6.5: Demonstrate proficiency with Incident Reviews

### LES 6.1: Management & Oversight

**Key Concepts:**

- ITSM Management & Oversight: Perform searches for PBIs awaiting assignment/review, or open for 30+ days

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 6.1.1: Identify investigations awaiting assignment
- ELO 6.1.2: Identify investigative tasks awaiting review
- ELO 6.1.3: Identify assigned investigative tasks without demonstrable progress

### LES 6.2: Quality Control

**Key Concepts:**

- PBI Quality Control: Perform five Quality Control checks on a newly created PBI

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 6.2.1: Demonstrate ability to review and validate newly created investigations and investigative tasking

### LES 6.3: Reporting

**Key Concepts:**

- Report & Message QC: Perform a Quality Control check for five record messages

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 6.3.1: Demonstrate ability to ensure investigative record keeping adheres to form and standard

### LAB 6.4: Turnover & Briefing

**Key Concepts:**

- Watch Turnover Briefings: Prepare a watch turnover briefing

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 6.4.1: Demonstrate ability to create an IH watch turnover briefing

### LAB 6.5: Incident Review

**Key Concepts:**

- PBI Processing Review: Review five open PBIs to determine proper processing

**Learning Objectives:**

- ELO 6.5.1: Demonstrate ability to monitor and assess investigative tasking and record keeping adhere to form and standard while investigations are ongoing
